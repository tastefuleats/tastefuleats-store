The Story Behind the Recipe

Lasagna was my first taste of edible grief. I remember making it after a breakup, layering noodles like emotional bandages, stirring sauce like a ritual. It was messy, saucy, and unapologetically comforting. Like therapy, but with garlic.

But the traditional version started to feel heavy. So I made my own. Not to copy—but to heal. To build a vegan lasagna that holds grief and garlic. That layers flavor with intention and comfort with clarity.

This version starts with a homemade Beyond-style ground—mushrooms, lentils, walnuts, and umami seasoning. Then comes the marinara, simmered with garlic and emotional depth. Then the cashew béchamel—creamy, nutty, and built to soothe. We layer it all between no-boil noodles and roast it until bubbling.

I’ve served it at rooftop hangs, post-therapy snack rituals, and chaotic brunch spreads. I’ve added spinach, swapped sauces, and even turned it into lasagna rolls. Every time, it hits. Every time, someone says, “This is better than real lasagna.”

So here it is. Layered, Saucy, Uncompromising. A vegan lasagna reboot that holds grief and garlic. Serve it hot. Eat it slow. And don’t forget the napkins—this dish gets emotional.

Ingredients

Vegan Ground

  • 1 cup mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cooked lentils
  • 1/2 cup walnuts
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Cashew Béchamel

  • 1 cup soaked cashews
  • 1/2 cup almond or oat milk
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt to taste

Marinara & Assembly

  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 9 no-boil lasagna noodles
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté mushrooms, lentils, and walnuts with soy sauce and spices until browned.
  2. Blend cashew béchamel ingredients until smooth. Adjust salt and lemon to taste.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F. In a baking dish, layer marinara, noodles, ground, béchamel. Repeat.
  4. Top with béchamel and fresh basil. Cover and bake 30 minutes. Uncover and bake 10 more.
  5. Let rest 10 minutes. Serve warm with sanctuary energy and garlic-forward healing.
