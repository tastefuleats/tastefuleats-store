The Hoodie That Honors Carbs, Chaos & Culinary Clarity

It started as a defense mechanism. Someone made a comment—half-joke, half-judgment—about carbs. About bread. About how “people like you” shouldn’t eat so much of it. I don’t remember the exact words. I just rememberabout carbs. About bread. About how “people like you” shouldn’t eat so much of it. I don’t remember the exact words. I just remember the feeling. That quiet shame. That internal flinch. That moment where joy got questioned.

So I said it. Loudly. Clearly. With sauce on my shirt and crumbs on my lips: “Don’t get it twisted—I love bread.”

And everyone laughed. But I didn’t say it for laughs. I said it for truth. For softness. For rebellion. For every person who’s ever been told to shrink their appetite, flatten their flavor, or apologize for loving something that nourishes them.

That phrase became a hoodie. But not just any hoodie. A hoodie that holds space for snack maximalism, emotional fluency, and unapologetic carb worship.

Bread Is More Than Bread

Bread isn’t just food. It’s memory. It’s ritual. It’s resistance. It’s the smell of sourdough proofing overnight. The sound of pita puffing in a hot pan. The feel of challah dough braided with grief and garlic. The taste of toast slathered with butter and regret.

Bread is emotional architecture. It’s edible intimacy. It’s the thing we reach for when we don’t know what else to say.

To love bread is to love softness. To love comfort. To love the parts of ourselves that crave warmth and texture and chew. It’s not just about carbs—it’s about connection.
