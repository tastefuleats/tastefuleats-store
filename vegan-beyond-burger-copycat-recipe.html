The Story Behind the Recipe

The first time I had a Beyond Burger, I was sitting cross-legged on a patch of grass at Outside Lands, glitter on my cheeks and fries in my lap. Someone handed me a burger wrapped in wax paper, and I took a bite without asking what it was. It was juicy, smoky, and weirdly nostalgic—like a backyard BBQ but with better music and fewer dads yelling about propane. That bite changed everything. I wasn’t vegan. I wasn’t even trying to be. But that burger made me rethink flavor, rethink indulgence, rethink what it meant to eat with intention. It wasn’t about sacrifice—it was about swagger. About showing up to the cookout with something that slaps and still respects the planet. So I started experimenting. I wanted a burger that hit the same notes: charred edges, juicy center, savory depth. I wanted something that could hold up to pickles, onions, and a messy sauce without falling apart. I wanted something that made people say, “Wait, this is vegan?” This recipe is the result. It’s built from mushrooms, lentils, and umami magic. It’s got smoked paprika, soy sauce, and a splash of liquid smoke. It’s pan-seared to perfection and stacked like it’s going on stage. It’s not trying to be meat—it’s trying to be legendary. I’ve served it at rooftop dinners, park picnics, and post-yoga brunches. I’ve watched meat-lovers go silent after the first bite. I’ve seen vegans cry. I’ve seen skeptics convert. This burger doesn’t ask for permission. It just shows up. And the best part? You can customize it. Add avocado, swap buns, go wild with sauces. It’s a canvas for chaos. A playground for flavor. A burger that doesn’t just feed you—it flexes with you. So here it is. Juicy, smoky, unapologetically plant-based. A copycat that doesn’t copy—it reinvents. Serve it with fries, glitter, and a playlist that slaps.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp liquid smoke
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for pan-searing

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mash lentils and mushrooms until chunky but cohesive.
  2. Add oats, soy sauce, tomato paste, spices, and liquid smoke. Mix well.
  3. Form into patties and chill for 30 minutes.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and sear patties 3–4 minutes per side until crispy.
  5. Serve on toasted buns with your favorite toppings. Flex hard.
