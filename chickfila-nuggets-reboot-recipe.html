Tender, Saucy, Unholy

Chick-fil-A nuggets were my first taste of snack devotion. I remember sneaking a tray into a movie theater, dipping in secret, chewing in reverence. They were tender, golden, and somehow spiritual. Like communion, but with Polynesian sauce.

But the chain version started to feel sanctimonious. So I made my own. Not to copy—but to convert. To build nuggets that are tender, saucy, and unholy in the best way. I wanted a bite that felt indulgent without compromise, a snack that could headline a brunch or fuel a rooftop hang.

This version starts with brined chicken breast chunks, dredged in a seasoned flour blend and fried until golden. The flavor? Slightly sweet, subtly spiced, and engineered for sauce worship. Serve with honey mustard, spicy mayo, or your own heretical creation. The crunch is unapologetic, the tenderness engineered for devotion, the sauce a sermon in every dip.

I’ve served them at rooftop hangs, post-rant snack attacks, and chaotic brunch spreads. I’ve tossed them in buffalo, glazed them in gochujang, and even turned them into nugget tacos. Once, I coated them in sesame seeds and called them “Snack Confessions.” Another time, I layered them into mac and cheese for “Unholy Comfort.” Every time, they hit. Every time, someone says, “These are better than Chick-fil-A.”

What makes them unholy isn’t just the flavor—it’s the ritual. The hiss of oil, the smell of fried devotion, the sight of sauce dripping down fingers. It’s the laughter of friends dipping together, the pride of reclaiming flavor, the joy of indulgence without compromise. Nuggets become more than food—they become memory, mood, and manifesto.

So here it is. Tender, Saucy, Unholy. A nugget reboot that converts the skeptics. Serve them hot. Dip them proud. And don’t forget the sauce—it’s part of the sermon. Because sometimes, the boldest bites are the ones that rewrite the rules of devotion.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup pickle juice (for brining)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Optional: dipping sauces (honey mustard, spicy mayo, ranch)

Instructions

  1. Brine chicken in pickle juice and milk for 30 minutes.
  2. Mix flour, powdered sugar, and spices in a bowl.
  3. Drain chicken, dredge in flour mixture, and let rest 10 minutes.
  4. Fry in hot oil until golden and cooked through. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve hot with sauces and snack reverence.
